Career

SOC (Security Operations Centre) Analyst A SOC Analyst monitors, detects, investigates, and responds to cybersecurity threats and incidents in real time. They work in a Security Operations Centre environment, acting as the first line of defence for an organisation's digital assets.

Key Job Responsibilities

Monitoring

  • Monitor security alerts and logs using SIEM tools (e.g., Splunk, Microsoft Sentinel)
  • Triage and investigate security incidents (Tier 1/2/3 analysis)

Analysis

  • Analyse network traffic, endpoint logs, and threat intelligence feeds
  • Escalate confirmed threats to senior analysts or incident response teams

Documentation

  • Document incidents, write post-incident reports, and update runbooks
  • Perform vulnerability scans and assist with patch management

Collaboration

  • Collaborate with IT and management on security policies and awareness

Relevance of SOC in Modern Cybersecurity

Continuous Security Monitoring

SOC teams provide 24/7 monitoring of networks, systems, and applications. This helps organizations detect suspicious activities and security incidents in real time.

Rapid Incident Detection and Response

SOC analysts identify threats such as malware, ransomware, phishing attacks, and unauthorized access. Quick response minimizes damage, reduces downtime, and prevents data breaches.

Protection Against Advanced Cyber Threats

Modern attackers use advanced techniques to bypass traditional security controls. SOCs utilize tools such as Security Information and Event Management (SIEM), threat intelligence, and endpoint detection solutions to identify complex attacks.

Compliance and Risk Management

Many industries must comply with cybersecurity regulations and standards. SOCs help organizations maintain compliance by monitoring security events and maintaining audit records.

This course covers essential topics including:
